Ever-changing Discoveries

Between 1491 and 1877, the science of ocean navigation transformed how people explored, claimed, and understood the Americas. From European expansion to American independence, advancements in navigation not only mapped coastlines — they mapped identity. This digital museum explores how scientific discovery at sea shaped who was considered “American,” who held power, and how those ideas reached the Gulf Coast and Houston region.
